L3 Students address the director about some important concerns

Universities are not just institutions of education, they are dynamic communities where students and administrators work together to create an enriching academic experience. Recently, the delegates of L3 groups have voiced concerns about certain university matters, sparking a dialogue about the importance of collaboration and open communication between students and administration. This article aims to delve into these concerns, emphasizing the need for a strengthened partnership between students and the university.

The full letter written by L3 delegates:

Dear Professor Baiche ,
We hope this message finds you well. We, delegates of L3 groups are writing to bring to your attention a set of concerns that we believe require immediate attention.

1. Unannounced Module Addition:
Students were surprised by the addition of a new module without prior notice which was unprofessional. Requesting transparency in such changes is essential to allow for adequate preparation.

2. Project Grading Adjustment:
The recent shift in project grading from 14 to the teacher and 6 to judges to 12 to the teacher and 8 to judges is causing dissatisfaction among students. We propose reconsideration to maintain fairness in assessment.

We understand the complexities of managing a large institution like ours, and we appreciate your dedication to ensuring its success. We believe that addressing these concerns will contribute to the overall well-being and effectiveness of our university.
We would be grateful for the opportunity to discuss these matters further at your convenience.
Thank you for your time and consideration.

Sincerely,
Delegates of L3.

L3 groups delegates have shared some worries, but it's not just about complaining. They are asking for a friendly conversation and positive changes. The people who run the institute, like the administrators, have a big job in making sure students have a good time learning. When they listen and respond to these worries, it shows they care about the students.

In summary, making sure the university is fair and open is something we all need to keep doing. Students and the people who run the institute should talk openly, be ready to fix problems, and make sure everyone is treated fairly. This helps us build a strong and happy academic community. By doing this together, we can make sure our institute stays a great place for students to learn, grow, and succeed.

QF5F+G3G, Boumerdes, Algeria
