About

IGEE

The National Institute of Electronics and Electrical Enginnering (INELEC) was established in 1976 in Boumerdes through an agreement between the National Society for the Manufacturing and Assembly of Electrical and Electronic Equipment (SONELEC) and the Education Development Center (EDC) based in Newton, Massachusetts, United States.

The objective of this accreditation was to establish an institute that provides comprehensive training in electrical and electronic engineering, drawing inspiration from American institutions of higher education.

A consortium of American universities supported the creation of INELEC, and the notable institutions involved in this collaboration were:

- Case Western Reserve University, Cleveland, Ohio.

- Oklahoma State University, Stillwater, Oklahoma.

- Stevens Institute of Technology.

- University of Houston, Houston, Texas.

- University of Missouri-Rolla, Rolla, Missouri.

- University of Wisconsin Stout, Menomonie, Wisconsin.

- Wentworth Institute of Technology, Boston, Massachusetts.

- Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT), Cambridge, Massachusetts.

- Purdue University, West Lafayette, Indiana.

INELEC transformed into the Department of Electrical and Electronic Engineering (DGEE) within the Faculty of Engineering Sciences (FSI) at the University of M'hamed Bougara Boumerdes (UMBB), while maintaining the American higher education methodology and English as the language of instruction.

In 2010, DGEE was elevated to the status of an institute. The Institute of Electrical and Electronic Engineering (IGEE) adopted the LMD system, gained financial independence, but continued to operate under the supervision of UMBB.
